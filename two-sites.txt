Yes, **they can absolutely work in tandem** and this is actually a very powerful strategy in digital marketing! Operating two distinct websites for different services is known as a "multi-site" or "niche-site" strategy. 

Because `i-smile.gr` (focusing on general/aesthetic dentistry like whitening and aligners) and `dentplant.gr` (focusing on implants and oral surgery) target different services, they will not hinder each other. Instead, they can dominate their respective niches.

Here is a breakdown of why this works, the benefits, and the few rules you need to follow to ensure they don't drag each other down:

### 🌟 The Benefits of a Two-Site Strategy

1. **Hyper-Focused SEO Authority:** Google prefers websites that are highly specialized. A site dedicated 100% to dental implants (`dentplant.gr`) will rank much faster for "implant surgeon Palaio Faliro" than a generic dental site that only has one page about implants. 

2. **No Keyword Cannibalization:** Because the sites offer different services, they aren't competing for the same search terms. `i-smile.gr` will capture people searching for "teeth whitening," while `dentplant.gr` will capture people searching for "bone grafting" or "implants."

3. **Targeted Design & User Experience:** The psychology mapping is different. A patient looking for a cosmetic smile makeover wants to see a design that is bright, fresh, and modern. A patient looking for a surgical implant needs a design that feels clinical, authoritative, and trustworthy. Having two sites allows for distinct branding.

4. **Cross-Linking Power:** You can use each site to boost the other. You can place a banner on `i-smile.gr` that says *"Need surgical solutions or implants? Visit our specialized implant center at Dentplant.gr,"* passing valuable SEO authority (link juice) between your own properties.

### ⚠️ The Rules to Prevent Them from Hindering Each Other

To make sure Google doesn't penalize you, there are three golden rules you must follow:

#### 1. Zero Duplicate Content (Crucial)
Never copy and paste paragraphs of text from `i-smile.gr` to `dentplant.gr`. Even the "About the Doctor" page should be written slightly differently. On `i-smile`, Dr. Moshopoulos's bio should emphasize his experience in aesthetics; on `dentplant`, it should emphasize his surgical background at Semmelweis and McGill.

#### 2. Clear Google Business Profile (Maps) Strategy
This is the only tricky part. Google Maps does not like two different businesses sharing the exact same address (`Πλατεία Ντάβαρη 2`) and phone number (`210 931 2651`). 
*   **The Best Approach:** Have **one** master Google Business Profile named something like *"Άγγελος Μοσχόπουλος | i-smile & Dentplant"*. Point the main "Website" button on Google Maps to your most profitable or primary site (e.g., `dentplant.gr`), but use the Google Business "Products/Services" tab to link out to *both* websites depending on the specific service the user clicks on.

#### 3. Separate Schema Identities 
In our recent SEO work, we made sure the `dentplant.gr` Schema Markup declares its own `@id`, explicitly telling Google it is a distinct digital entity from `i-smile.gr`, even though they share the same doctor and address.

### The Verdict
You are in a great position. By keeping `i-smile.gr` focused on aesthetics and `dentplant.gr` focused on implants, you are casting a much wider and more effective net than a single, crowded website ever could.
